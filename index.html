About me

I have a background in Psychology, and I am currently following a master in Methodology and Statistics with focus on the behavioral and social sciences. For my master thesis I investigate Bayesian Evidence Synthesis, which aims to aggregate evidence across conceptual replications, and thus, to test the veracity and the generalizability of theories. Apart from statistical research, I am growing more and more interested in global societal challenges and how we can strive to tackle them with interdisciplinary research. For this reason, I am following the Graduate Honours Interdisciplinary Seminars, which allow me to take a sneak peek into various disciplines. While I am very passionate about research on its own, I would also enjoy engaging with professionals outside of academia and facilitate the communication and application of scientific findings.
